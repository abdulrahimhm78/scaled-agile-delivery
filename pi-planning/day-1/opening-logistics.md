# Day 1 – Opening & Logistics (PI Planning)

## Purpose
Establish a structured, disciplined start to PI Planning by aligning participants, setting expectations, confirming logistics, and ensuring readiness for effective collaboration over the two-day event.

---

## Objectives
- Ensure all participants understand the PI Planning goals, agenda, and outcomes
- Confirm roles, responsibilities, and decision authorities
- Validate logistics, tooling, and communication norms
- Create a focused, professional environment that supports execution

---

## Audience
- Release Train Engineer (RTE)
- Scrum Masters
- Product Owners / Product Managers
- Business Owners
- System Architects / Engineering Leads
- Delivery and Program Leadership
- Supporting stakeholders (as applicable)

---

## Timebox
**30–45 minutes** (strictly enforced)

---

## Agenda

### 1. Welcome & Opening Remarks
**Owner:** RTE / Program Leadership  
- Formal welcome to all participants  
- Reinforce the importance of PI Planning as a commitment-driven event  
- Emphasize outcomes over activities  

---

### 2. PI Planning Purpose & Success Criteria
**Owner:** RTE  
- Why this PI Planning matters now  
- How success will be measured:
  - Aligned PI Objectives
  - Identified and managed dependencies
  - Transparent risks and mitigation plans
  - Realistic, capacity-based commitments  

---

### 3. Agenda & Event Flow Overview
**Owner:** RTE  
- Walk through Day 1 and Day 2 flow at a high level  
- Highlight key milestones:
  - Business Context
  - Architecture Vision
  - Team Breakouts
  - Draft and Final PI Objectives
  - Risk Review (ROAM)
- Reinforce timebox discipline  

---

### 4. Roles & Responsibilities
**Owner:** RTE  

Clarify expectations for each role:

- **RTE**
  - Facilitation, flow, and escalation
- **Product Owners / Product Managers**
  - Priority clarity, scope decisions, PI Objectives
- **Scrum Masters**
  - Team execution support, dependency tracking, risk surfacing
- **Business Owners**
  - Strategic alignment, objective scoring, decision support
- **Architects / Engineering Leads**
  - Architectural guidance, feasibility validation
- **Teams**
  - Honest capacity planning and delivery commitments  

---

### 5. Logistics & Working Agreements
**Owner:** RTE / Scrum Masters  

Cover explicitly:

- **Location / Rooms**
  - Team breakout rooms
  - Shared sessions location
- **Timing**
  - Start/end times
  - Breaks and lunch
- **Tools**
  - Program board
  - Planning tools (e.g., Jira, Azure DevOps, Miro)
  - Communication channels (Teams/Slack)
- **Documentation**
  - Where PI Objectives, risks, and dependencies are recorded
- **Decision Protocol**
  - Who decides, how decisions are documented
- **Escalation Path**
  - How blockers and risks are raised during the event  

---

### 6. Norms & Expectations
**Owner:** RTE  

Set clear behavioral standards:

- Cameras on (for virtual/hybrid)
- Be present and engaged
- Side conversations taken offline
- Raise risks early, not late
- Commit only to what is achievable
- Transparency over optimism  

---

### 7. Readiness Check
**Owner:** Scrum Masters  

Confirm readiness across teams:

- Team members present and accounted for
- Backlogs refined to an acceptable level
- Capacity assumptions validated
- Known constraints surfaced
- Dependencies preliminarily identified  

Any readiness gaps are logged and addressed immediately.

---

### 8. Transition to Business Context
**Owner:** RTE  

- Close opening session
- Confirm next speaker and timing
- Reinforce focus on outcomes as the event progresses

---

## Outputs
- Shared understanding of PI Planning goals and flow
- Confirmed logistics and working agreements
- Clear role accountability
- Identified readiness gaps (if any) with owners assigned

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Rushing through logistics and creating confusion later
- Unclear decision authority
- Overloading the opening with detail instead of alignment
- Allowing late arrivals to disrupt flow

---

## Guiding Principle
A disciplined opening creates disciplined execution.  
Clarity at the start prevents chaos during planning.
